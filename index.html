Alfredo Stroessner

Paraguayan army officer and politician who served as President of Paraguay

Time line of Stroessner's early life

  • 1912 - Born in Encarnación, Paraguay
  • 1929 - Enrolled in the Francisco López Military Academy
  • 1931 - Received his commission as a lieutenant in the Paraguayan Army
  • 1932 - Fought against Bolivian forces in the Battle of Boquerón during the Chaco War
  • 1940 - He had risen to the rank of major and joined the general staff
  • 1947 - Commanded the artillery division at Paraguarí that ensured that President Higinio Morínigo won the war by destroying a working-class rebel area of Asunción, when the Paraguayan Civil War broke out
  • 1948 - Became a brigadier and the youngest general officer in South America. His accurate political sense failed him only once, when he found himself on the wrong side of a failed coup attempt and had to be driven to the Brazilian embassy in the trunk of a car, earning him the nickname of "Colonel Trunk". Stroessner backed Felipe Molas López in a successful coup against Juan Natalicio González. He then backed Federico Chávez against Molas López
  • 1951 - Became commander-in-chief of the Armed forces of Paraguay
